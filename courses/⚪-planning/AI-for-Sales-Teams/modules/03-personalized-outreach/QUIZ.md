## Answer Key

### Section 1: Multiple Choice

1. **B** - Research-based insights specific to their situation
   - *Genuine personalization shows understanding of their specific context.*

2. **B** - 75-125 words
   - *Short enough to read quickly, long enough to provide value.*

3. **C** - To demonstrate research and relevance to earn the rest of the email being read
   - *The opening must earn attention before you can deliver your message.*

4. **B** - Personalized: Reference to their specific situation or trigger event
   - *Personalization increases open rates by signaling relevance.*

5. **C** - Pitch your product or service
   - *Connection requests should focus on connection, not selling.*

6. **B** - Helpful early, more direct later, professional close at end
   - *Natural progression from informative to action-oriented.*

7. **B** - The final email in a sequence that closes the loop professionally
   - *Break-up emails acknowledge lack of response and close professionally.*

8. **B** - 300 characters
   - *LinkedIn limits connection request notes to 300 characters.*

9. **B** - Provide new value or a different angle
   - *Each touch should add something new, not just remind.*

10. **C** - Edit it to add your voice while keeping valuable elements
    - *AI provides drafts; you provide authenticity.*

### Section 2: True/False

11. **True** - Shorter subjects display better on mobile and typically have higher open rates.

12. **False** - Each follow-up should add new value; repetition is ineffective and annoying.

13. **False** - Personalization requires human review to ensure it's authentic to your voice and genuinely relevant.

14. **True** - LinkedIn is a more casual channel; messages should be concise (50-100 words typical).

15. **True** - Testing multiple variables simultaneously makes it impossible to know what caused results.

### Section 3: Scenario-Based

16. **B** - It's generic with no personalization or research demonstration
    - *"I hope this email finds you well" and generic value claims show no research.*

17. **B** - Provide new value (insight, resource, case study) with a clear ask
    - *Each follow-up must add value; "checking in" provides nothing.*

18. **B** - Your subject lines work but email content isn't compelling
    - *High opens + low replies = message isn't resonating.*

19. **C** - Provide different value and be slightly more direct
    - *Second message adds new value with firmer CTA.*

20. **B** - Remove "amazing" and make it more specific to their situation
    - *Avoid superlatives; add specificity for relevance.*
