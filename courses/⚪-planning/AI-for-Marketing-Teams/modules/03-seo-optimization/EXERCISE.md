## Part 1: Keyword Research (~40 minutes)

### Task

Build a comprehensive keyword map for one topic cluster.

### Step 1: Choose Your Topic (5 min)

Select a topic relevant to your brand:
- A product/service you offer
- A problem your audience faces
- An industry trend

**Your topic**: _________________

### Step 2: Seed Keyword Expansion (15 min)

Use this prompt:

```
ROLE: SEO strategist for [your industry].

SEED KEYWORDS:
1. [Your main keyword]
2. [Related keyword]
3. [Problem keyword]

TASK: Expand into 50 keyword opportunities.

FOR EACH, provide:
- Keyword phrase
- Search intent (Informational/Commercial/Transactional)
- Difficulty estimate (Easy/Medium/Hard)
- Content type (Blog/Guide/Landing page/FAQ)
- Priority (High/Medium/Low)

ORGANIZE by:
1. Questions (how, what, why, when, can, does)
2. Comparisons (vs, alternative, best)
3. Problem-focused
4. Solution-focused
5. Long-tail specific
```

### Step 3: Question Mining (10 min)

```
Generate 20 questions real people ask about [topic].

Categorize by:
- AWARENESS: Just discovering the problem
- CONSIDERATION: Researching solutions
- DECISION: Ready to choose/buy

For each: Question + Best content format to answer it
```

### Step 4: Keyword Clustering (10 min)

```
Group these keywords into content clusters:

[Paste your 50 keywords]

Create 5-7 clusters with:
- Cluster name
- Primary keyword
- Supporting keywords
- Recommended pillar content
- Recommended supporting content
```

### Deliverables for Part 1

- [ ] 50 keywords organized by type
- [ ] 20 questions organized by stage
- [ ] 5-7 keyword clusters with content recommendations

## Part 3: Content Brief Creation (~30 minutes)

### Task

Create a comprehensive SEO content brief for a new article.

### Step 1: Generate the Brief (20 min)

```
ROLE: SEO content strategist.

TARGET KEYWORD: [Your primary keyword]
SECONDARY KEYWORDS: [3-5 from your research]
SEARCH INTENT: [From your analysis]
WORD COUNT TARGET: [Based on competitors]

CREATE A CONTENT BRIEF:

1. TITLE OPTIONS (5)
   - Include keyword naturally
   - Under 60 characters
   - Power words included

2. META DESCRIPTION (3)
   - Under 155 characters
   - Keyword included
   - CTA included

3. ARTICLE STRUCTURE
   - H1 recommendation
   - H2 sections (based on competitor analysis + gaps)
   - H3 subsections where needed
   - Introduction angle
   - Conclusion approach

4. CONTENT REQUIREMENTS
   - Key points per section
   - Data/statistics needed
   - Examples to include
   - Questions to answer (from research)

5. SEO CHECKLIST
   - Keyword placement guide
   - Internal linking opportunities
   - External linking suggestions
   - Featured snippet opportunities

6. UNIQUE VALUE
   - What makes this better than competitors
   - Original elements to include
   - Expert insights to add
```

### Step 2: Refine and Validate (10 min)

Review your brief against:
- [ ] Does it address the search intent fully?
- [ ] Does it include your competitive advantages?
- [ ] Is the structure logical and scannable?
- [ ] Are there clear differentiation points?
- [ ] Would this satisfy a searcher completely?

### Deliverables for Part 3

- [ ] Complete content brief
- [ ] 5 title options
- [ ] 3 meta descriptions
- [ ] Full article structure

## Part 4: Content Optimization (~30 minutes)

### Task

Optimize an existing piece of content using AI analysis.

### Step 1: Select Content

Use your own content OR this sample:

<summary>Sample Content to Optimize (Click to expand)</summary>

```
Title: Marketing Tips for Small Businesses

Marketing is important for small businesses. Here are some tips
to help you market your business better.

Social Media Marketing
Social media is a great way to reach customers. You should post
regularly on platforms like Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn.
Make sure your posts are engaging and relevant to your audience.

Email Marketing
Email is still effective for marketing. Build an email list and
send regular newsletters to your subscribers. Include valuable
content and occasional promotions.

Content Marketing
Creating content helps establish your expertise. Write blog posts,
create videos, or start a podcast. Consistency is key.

SEO
Search engine optimization helps people find you online. Use
relevant keywords in your content and make sure your website is
fast and mobile-friendly.

Conclusion
Marketing doesn't have to be complicated. Start with one or two
channels and expand as you grow. The most important thing is to
be consistent and provide value to your audience.
```

### Step 2: AI Optimization Analysis (15 min)

```
ROLE: SEO content analyst.

TARGET KEYWORD: "small business marketing tips"
CURRENT CONTENT:
[Paste the content]

COMPREHENSIVE AUDIT:

1. KEYWORD OPTIMIZATION (Score 1-10)
   - Primary keyword usage
   - Secondary keyword opportunities
   - Natural placement suggestions

2. CONTENT DEPTH (Score 1-10)
   - Missing topics
   - Sections to expand
   - Questions left unanswered

3. STRUCTURE (Score 1-10)
   - Heading optimization
   - Paragraph length
   - List opportunities
   - Table opportunities

4. ENGAGEMENT (Score 1-10)
   - Opening hook quality
   - Scanability
   - CTA effectiveness
   - Internal/external link opportunities

5. HELPFULNESS (Score 1-10)
   - Does it satisfy search intent?
   - Unique value provided
   - Expertise demonstrated

PROVIDE:
- Overall score
- Top 5 priority improvements
- Rewritten sections for worst areas
```

### Step 3: Implement Improvements (15 min)

Based on the audit, request specific rewrites:

```
Rewrite the [weakest section] with:
- More specific, actionable advice
- A concrete example
- Keyword optimization for "[keyword]"
- Improved structure with subheadings
- Original insight or data point
```

Apply REVIEW and finalize.
