## Answer Key

### Section 1: Multiple Choice

1. **B** - AI augments human agents, handling routine tasks while humans manage complex issues
   - *The augmentation model is the most effective and realistic approach.*

2. **B** - Matching tickets to agents based on their expertise and capabilities
   - *Skills-based routing improves FCR by connecting issues to qualified agents.*

3. **B** - Combining search results with AI generation to provide accurate, sourced responses
   - *RAG grounds AI responses in actual documentation to reduce hallucinations.*

4. **B** - The percentage of conversations fully resolved without human handoff
   - *Containment rate measures chatbot self-service effectiveness.*

5. **B** - 100% conversation coverage vs. sampling 2-5% of interactions
   - *AI enables reviewing every interaction, not just a statistical sample.*

6. **B** - Breaking down sentiment by specific topics (pricing, support quality, product)
   - *Aspect-based analysis provides actionable insights by topic.*

7. **B** - Database of previously approved translations for consistency
   - *Translation memory ensures consistent terminology across interactions.*

8. **B** - Early adopters who help promote and support AI adoption among peers
   - *Champions are peer influencers who model successful adoption.*

9. **C** - Inability to process text-based queries
   - *AI excels at text processing; the other options are real limitations.*

10. **B** - When detecting frustration, complex issues, or explicit requests for human help
    - *Escalation should be contextual, not rule-based on turns.*

11. **B** - Regularly comparing AI scores to human evaluator scores and adjusting
    - *Calibration ensures AI and human scoring remain aligned.*

12. **B** - Agent-assistance tools first (lower risk), then customer-facing AI
    - *Starting lower-risk builds confidence before higher-stakes deployment.*

### Section 2: True/False

13. **True** - 85%+ accuracy is a common threshold for automated routing trust.

14. **False** - Suggestions should always be reviewed and personalized by agents before sending.

15. **False** - Honest acknowledgment of limitations builds trust; saying "I don't know, but I can connect you to someone who does" is better than wrong answers.

16. **True** - Real-time sentiment enables proactive supervisor intervention during conversations.

17. **False** - Human review is still needed for quality, nuance, and cultural appropriateness.

18. **True** - Leading indicators predict success during implementation; lagging indicators measure outcomes after.

19. **False** - AI should augment human QA, not replace it; human judgment is still needed for edge cases and calibration.

20. **False** - Augmentation means AI assists humans across all interactions; even "easy" tickets benefit from human oversight.

### Section 3: Scenario-Based

21. **B** - Keep AI routing for high-confidence predictions, use human triage for low-confidence
    - *Confidence-based workflows maximize AI value while managing risk.*

22. **B** - Knowledge gaps - audit and update the knowledge base feeding the AI
    - *AI suggestions are only as good as the knowledge they're trained on.*

23. **B** - Improve intent recognition and add clarifying question flows
    - *The feedback indicates intent classification problems, not knowledge gaps.*

24. **B** - The scoring criteria may not align with what customers actually value
    - *When AI QA and CSAT diverge, the rubric likely needs adjustment.*

25. **B** - Investigate root cause (staffing, technical issue, volume spike) and address
    - *Sentiment signals should trigger investigation, not panic or dismissal.*

26. **B** - Start with translated knowledge base, but have native speakers review AI outputs before expanding
    - *Phased approach with quality gates manages risk while enabling progress.*

27. **B** - Investigate the resistant agent's specific concerns - they may have identified real issues
    - *Resistance often reveals legitimate problems worth addressing.*

28. **B** - "There's no single number; we track a balanced scorecard of efficiency, quality, and adoption metrics"
    - *Single metrics can be gamed; balanced scorecards tell the full story.*

29. **B** - Proactively offer human agent transfer with empathy acknowledgment
    - *Proactive escalation based on sentiment improves customer experience.*

30. **B** - Results are tracking to plan if Year 1 investment was expected to be net-negative with payback in Year 2
    - *Year 1 losses are normal for AI implementations due to setup costs and adoption curves.*
