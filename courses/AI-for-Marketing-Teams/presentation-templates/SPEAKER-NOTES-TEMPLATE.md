## Module 1 Example Notes

### SLIDE 1: AI Fundamentals for Marketers

**TALKING POINTS:**
- Welcome participants, introduce yourself
- Explain this is the foundation module
- Set expectations: practical skills, not just theory

**KEY MESSAGE:**
By the end of this session, you'll have the confidence to use AI tools effectively in your daily marketing work.

**TRANSITION:**
"Before we dive into the tools, let's look at what we're actually dealing with..."

**TIME:** 2 minutes

---

### SLIDE 2: The Marketing AI Landscape

**TALKING POINTS:**
- AI isn't one thing - it's an ecosystem of tools
- Focus on what matters for marketers: text generation, image creation, analysis
- Don't get overwhelmed - we'll narrow down to what's practical

**STORIES/EXAMPLES:**
"When I first started using AI for marketing, I tried to learn everything at once. Big mistake. The teams that succeed pick 1-2 tools and master them."

**KEY MESSAGE:**
You don't need to know everything about AI - just what works for marketing.

**TRANSITION:**
"So how do these tools actually work? Let me give you the 30-second explanation..."

**TIME:** 3 minutes

---

### SLIDE 3: How AI Actually Works

**TALKING POINTS:**
- Keep it simple - pattern prediction, not thinking
- This explains why AI makes mistakes
- Understanding this helps you use it better

**ANALOGIES TO USE:**
"Think of it like autocomplete on steroids. Your phone predicts the next word. AI predicts the next sentence, paragraph, or page."

**ANTICIPATE QUESTIONS:**
Q: "Is it creative?"
A: "It recombines patterns in novel ways. Whether that's 'creative' is philosophical, but it can produce useful variations."

**KEY MESSAGE:**
AI predicts what text should come next based on patterns. It doesn't understand meaning.

**TRANSITION:**
"Knowing this helps us understand both what AI does well and where it falls short..."

**TIME:** 3 minutes

---

### SLIDE 4: What AI Does Well

**TALKING POINTS:**
- First drafts - getting past the blank page
- Variations - multiple options quickly
- Format conversion - same content, different format
- Summarization - extracting key points

**DEMONSTRATION OPPORTUNITY:**
Show a quick example: "Give me 5 headline variations for [topic]" - takes 10 seconds

**REAL EXAMPLE:**
"One of my clients reduced their content production time by 60% by using AI for first drafts. But - and this is important - they still edit everything."

**KEY MESSAGE:**
AI excels at speed and scale for structured tasks.

**TRANSITION:**
"But it's equally important to know where AI falls short..."

**TIME:** 3 minutes

---

### SLIDE 5: Where AI Falls Short

**TALKING POINTS:**
- Factual accuracy - AI makes things up confidently
- True originality - it remixes, doesn't invent
- Brand voice - without training, it's generic
- Strategy - it can't make real decisions

**WARNING:**
"I cannot stress this enough: NEVER trust AI-generated statistics or URLs. Always verify."

**EXAMPLE:**
Share a specific hallucination you've encountered: made-up study, fake URL, etc.

**KEY MESSAGE:**
AI has real limitations. Knowing them prevents embarrassing mistakes.

**TRANSITION:**
"This is why the best results come from human-AI collaboration..."

**TIME:** 4 minutes

---

### SLIDE 11: The REVIEW Framework

**TALKING POINTS:**
- This is our quality control system
- Every piece of AI content goes through this
- Takes 5-10 minutes, saves hours of problems

**EMPHASIS:**
"This framework is what separates amateur AI users from professionals. Anyone can generate content. The skill is in the review."

**KEY MESSAGE:**
REVIEW framework: Read, Evaluate, Verify, Improve, Enhance, Watch

**TIME:** 2 minutes

---

### SLIDE 17: Watch for AI Tells

**TALKING POINTS:**
- These phrases scream "AI wrote this"
- Your audience notices, even unconsciously
- Search engines may also detect patterns

**INTERACTIVE ELEMENT:**
"Let's play a game: I'll show you two paragraphs. One is AI-generated with tells, one is edited. Can you spot the difference?"

**LIST TO SHARE:**
Red flags: "In today's fast-paced world," "It's important to note," "In conclusion," "crucial/vital/key"

**KEY MESSAGE:**
Remove robotic indicators to make AI content sound human.

**TIME:** 4 minutes

## Demo Notes Template

### Demo: [Demo Name]

**SETUP CHECKLIST:**
- [ ] AI tool logged in
- [ ] Browser tabs arranged
- [ ] Example content ready
- [ ] Backup screenshot if needed

**DEMO SCRIPT:**

1. **Open tool** (show login process briefly)
   - Narrate: "I'm using [tool] for this because..."

2. **Enter prompt** (type slowly so audience can follow)
   - Narrate: "Notice how I structure this with ROLE, CONTEXT, TASK..."

3. **Wait for response** (use wait time to explain)
   - Narrate: "While this generates, notice..."

4. **Review output** (scroll through together)
   - Narrate: "Let's apply REVIEW - starting with Read..."

5. **Make edits** (show actual changes)
   - Narrate: "Here I'm fixing [specific issue]..."

**IF DEMO FAILS:**
"Technology doesn't always cooperate! Let me show you the backup example, which demonstrates the same concept..."

**TIME:** 8-10 minutes

## Exercise Facilitation Notes

### Exercise: [Exercise Name]

**BEFORE STARTING:**
- Confirm everyone has tool access
- Share any needed resources
- Set clear expectations

**OPENING SCRIPT:**
"For the next [X] minutes, you'll be working on [task]. Your goal is to [specific outcome]. I'll be walking around to help."

**COMMON PROBLEMS:**
1. "I don't know what to write about"
   → Provide backup topic options
2. "My AI is giving bad output"
   → Check their prompt structure
3. "I'm stuck on step 2"
   → Pair them with someone who's progressing

**TIME CHECKS:**
- 5 min in: "Everyone should be past step 1"
- 10 min in: "You should be working on step 2 now"
- 5 min left: "Start wrapping up, we'll discuss in 5 minutes"

**DEBRIEF QUESTIONS:**
- "What worked well?"
- "What was challenging?"
- "What surprised you?"

## Handling Questions

### Anticipated Questions & Answers

**Q: "Which AI tool is best?"**
A: "It depends on your use case. For [scenario], I'd recommend [tool] because... Let's discuss your specific needs after the session."

**Q: "Will AI replace marketers?"**
A: "AI is replacing tasks, not jobs. Marketers who use AI will replace marketers who don't. This course is about being in the first group."

**Q: "Is AI content ethical?"**
A: "Great question. The key is transparency and value. Using AI to create helpful content faster is different from using it to deceive. We'll cover ethical guidelines."

**Q: "How do I get my boss to approve AI tools?"**
A: "Start small. Do a pilot project, track time saved, show quality comparison. We'll cover the business case in detail."

### When You Don't Know

"That's a great question, and I want to give you an accurate answer. Let me research that and get back to you by [timeframe]."

## Energy Management

### Signs Energy Is Dropping:
- Fewer questions
- Checking phones
- Glazed expressions

### Energy Boosters:
1. **Quick poll:** "Raise your hand if you've ever..."
2. **Partner discussion:** "Turn to your neighbor and discuss..."
3. **Movement:** "Stand up, stretch for 30 seconds"
4. **Story:** Share a relevant anecdote
5. **Demo:** Switch from slides to live tool

### Natural Break Points:
- After every 20-25 minutes of content
- After complex concepts
- Before exercises
- After exercises (debrief + break)

---

## Time Management

### If Running Behind:
- Skip extended examples (use backup deck)
- Combine similar exercises
- Assign detailed review as homework
- Focus on core concepts, defer advanced

### If Running Ahead:
- Add more Q&A time
- Do extended exercises
- Include optional demonstrations
- Deeper discussions on applications

### Non-Negotiable Content:
- Core framework/concept
- Main exercise
- Key takeaways
- Next steps

---

## Closing Notes

### Final 5 Minutes:
1. Summarize key takeaways (1 min)
2. Preview next module (30 sec)
3. Assign any homework (30 sec)
4. Share resources (1 min)
5. Final questions (2 min)

### Exit Script:
"Before you leave: your homework is [X]. Remember, the best way to learn AI is to use it - so try applying today's concepts before our next session. Any final questions? Great, see you in [next module]!"

*These notes are guides, not scripts. Adapt to your style and audience.*
