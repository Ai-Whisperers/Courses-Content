## Section: Keyword Research (7 questions)

### Q3.1
**What is "keyword clustering" in SEO?**

A) Using as many keywords as possible in one piece of content
B) Grouping related keywords into topical categories
C) Hiding keywords in the page code
D) Using only one keyword per page

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Keyword clustering organizes related search terms into groups that can be addressed together, helping create comprehensive content that ranks for multiple related terms.

---

### Q3.2
**How can AI assist with keyword research?**

A) AI can determine exact search volumes
B) AI can generate keyword ideas and group related terms
C) AI has real-time search data access
D) AI can guarantee rankings for specific keywords

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** AI is useful for brainstorming keyword ideas and organizing them into logical groups. However, actual search volume data requires dedicated SEO tools.

---

### Q3.3
**What is "search intent" and why does it matter for SEO?**

A) The number of times a keyword is searched
B) The underlying purpose behind a user's search query
C) How long someone stays on a page
D) The competition level for a keyword

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Search intent is what the user actually wants to accomplish. Content must match intent (informational, commercial, transactional, navigational) to rank and satisfy users.

---

### Q3.4
**What are the four main types of search intent?**

A) Fast, slow, medium, paused
B) Informational, commercial, transactional, navigational
C) Buying, selling, browsing, leaving
D) Local, global, regional, national

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** The four intent types are: Informational (learning), Commercial (researching options), Transactional (ready to buy), and Navigational (finding a specific page).

---

### Q3.5
**When using AI for keyword research, what's a critical limitation?**

A) AI can't spell keywords correctly
B) AI doesn't have access to real-time search volume data
C) AI can't understand keyword meanings
D) AI only works with English keywords

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** AI models don't have access to current search volume data. Use AI for ideation and grouping, but verify volumes with dedicated SEO tools like Semrush or Ahrefs.

---

### Q3.6
**What is a "long-tail keyword"?**

A) A keyword with many characters
B) A longer, more specific phrase with lower volume but higher intent
C) A keyword that takes a long time to rank for
D) A keyword used at the end of content

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Long-tail keywords are specific phrases (e.g., "best CRM for small law firms") with lower search volume but often higher conversion intent than broad terms.

---

### Q3.7
**How should AI-generated keyword lists be validated?**

A) Use them as-is since AI knows SEO
B) Cross-reference with SEO tools for volume, difficulty, and trends
C) Remove any keywords you haven't heard of
D) Only use the shortest keywords

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** AI-generated keywords need validation with actual SEO data to confirm they're worth targeting based on volume, competition, and business relevance.

## Section: Content Briefs (7 questions)

### Q3.8
**What should an AI-generated content brief include?**

A) Just the keyword and word count
B) Target keywords, outline, questions to answer, competitor analysis
C) Only the headline
D) A complete written article

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Comprehensive briefs include keywords, content structure, search intent, questions to address, competitor insights, and formatting requirements.

---

### Q3.9
**Why include competitor analysis in content briefs?**

A) To copy what competitors are doing
B) To understand what content already ranks and find gaps to fill
C) To avoid covering similar topics
D) To determine your page design

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Competitor analysis reveals what content satisfies search intent (ranking content) and where you can provide unique value (gaps).

---

### Q3.10
**What are "People Also Ask" questions and how are they useful?**

A) Questions competitors ask in their content
B) Search engine questions that reveal related topics users want answered
C) Survey questions for audience research
D) FAQ questions to avoid

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** "People Also Ask" shows related questions searchers have, helping you create comprehensive content that addresses the full topic.

---

### Q3.11
**How should word count be determined for AI content briefs?**

A) Always default to 2,000 words
B) Analyze top-ranking content length and match or exceed appropriately
C) Keep everything under 500 words for readability
D) The longer, the better, regardless of topic

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Optimal length depends on the topic and what currently ranks. Some queries need comprehensive coverage; others are better served concisely.

---

### Q3.12
**What makes a good content brief question list?**

A) Generic questions that apply to any topic
B) Specific questions your target audience asks about this topic
C) Questions that promote your products
D) Yes/no questions only

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Good questions reflect what your audience actually wants to know, helping ensure content addresses real user needs and search intent.

---

### Q3.13
**How should heading structure be specified in a content brief?**

A) Leave it up to the writer
B) Define H2 and H3 sections with target keywords integrated naturally
C) Use as many H1 tags as possible
D) Avoid headings for a cleaner look

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Heading structure should be planned with SEO in mind (one H1, logical H2/H3 hierarchy) with keywords integrated where natural.

---

### Q3.14
**What internal linking guidance should a content brief include?**

A) No linking needed
B) Relevant pages to link to and from, with suggested anchor text
C) Links to competitor sites
D) Maximum number of links allowed

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Internal linking helps SEO and user experience. Briefs should specify related content to connect and appropriate anchor text options.

## Section: On-Page Optimization (6 questions)

### Q3.15
**What's the recommended title tag length?**

A) Under 30 characters
B) 50-60 characters
C) 100+ characters
D) Exactly 75 characters

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Title tags should be 50-60 characters to display fully in search results. Longer titles get truncated.

---

### Q3.16
**How should AI be used for meta description writing?**

A) Let AI write one description and use it
B) Generate multiple options that include keywords and compelling CTAs
C) Skip meta descriptions since AI can't help
D) Copy the first paragraph of the article

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Use AI to generate multiple meta description options, then select and refine the best one that includes target keywords and encourages clicks.

---

### Q3.17
**What's the primary purpose of a meta description?**

A) To improve keyword rankings directly
B) To encourage clicks by communicating page value
C) To repeat the title tag
D) To list all page keywords

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Meta descriptions don't directly impact rankings but significantly influence click-through rates by selling the value of clicking on your result.

---

### Q3.18
**How should header tags (H1, H2, H3) be optimized for SEO?**

A) Use keywords in every header
B) Include keywords naturally in headers while maintaining readability
C) Avoid keywords in headers
D) Use only H1 tags throughout

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Headers should include keywords where they fit naturally, maintaining hierarchy (one H1, H2s for main sections, H3s for subsections).

---

### Q3.19
**What is E-E-A-T and why does it matter for AI content?**

A) A diet trend that Google follows
B) Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness - Google's quality guidelines
C) A content format requirement
D) The name of Google's ranking algorithm

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** E-E-A-T represents Google's quality standards. AI content should demonstrate human expertise and experience to meet these criteria.

---

### Q3.20
**How can you add E-E-A-T signals to AI-generated content?**

A) Mention AI wrote it
B) Add author credentials, original examples, and unique insights
C) Include more keywords
D) Make the content longer

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** E-E-A-T is improved by showing real expertise: author bios, personal experience, original research, and insights that demonstrate actual knowledge.
