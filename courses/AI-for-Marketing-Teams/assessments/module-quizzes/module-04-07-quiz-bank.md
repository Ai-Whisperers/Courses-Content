# Module 4: Visual Content Creation (15 questions)

## Section: AI Image Generation

### Q4.1
**What's the most important element of an image generation prompt?**

A) The name of the AI tool
B) Specific, descriptive details about the desired image
C) The image file format
D) The exact pixel dimensions

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Detailed prompts with specific descriptors (style, composition, colors, mood) produce better results than vague requests.

---

### Q4.2
**What should you include in image prompts for brand consistency?**

A) Nothing special - AI handles consistency
B) Color codes, style descriptors, and brand aesthetic keywords
C) Only your logo
D) The photographer's name

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Include specific brand elements (hex colors, style terms like "minimalist" or "modern," mood descriptors) for consistent visual output.

---

### Q4.3
**What's a common limitation of AI-generated images?**

A) They're always too dark
B) Text and fine details like hands/faces can be distorted
C) They only work in black and white
D) They can't include people

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** AI struggles with text in images, human hands, faces, and fine details. Review these elements carefully before use.

---

### Q4.4
**When should you NOT use AI-generated images?**

A) Social media posts
B) Blog headers
C) Images of real people or product photography
D) Concept visualization

**Correct Answer:** C
**Explanation:** AI shouldn't be used for images that need to show real people authentically or accurate product representations.

---

### Q4.5
**How can you improve AI image results through iteration?**

A) Use the same prompt repeatedly
B) Refine your prompt based on initial outputs, adjusting specific elements
C) Switch to a different AI tool each time
D) Add "better" to the prompt

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Study what worked/didn't work in outputs, then adjust specific descriptors to refine results.

---

# Module 5: Marketing Analytics (15 questions)

## Section: Data Analysis

### Q5.1
**How should you prepare data before uploading to AI for analysis?**

A) Upload raw, unformatted data
B) Clean, label columns clearly, and remove sensitive information
C) Convert everything to images
D) Encrypt all data

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Clean, well-labeled data with clear column headers produces better analysis. Remove PII and sensitive information.

---

### Q5.2
**What's a critical limitation when using AI for marketing data analysis?**

A) AI can't read spreadsheets
B) AI may misinterpret data context and make calculation errors
C) AI only works with small datasets
D) AI can't create charts

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** AI can misinterpret data relationships and make mathematical errors. Always verify key calculations manually.

---

### Q5.3
**How should you prompt AI for data analysis?**

A) "Analyze this data"
B) Specify the metrics, questions to answer, and context about what the data represents
C) Just upload the file
D) Ask for "insights"

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Provide context about what metrics mean, what questions you're trying to answer, and any relevant business context.

---

### Q5.4
**What should you do with surprising findings from AI analysis?**

A) Accept them immediately
B) Verify the calculation and check if the interpretation makes sense
C) Ignore them as errors
D) Report them without verification

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Surprising findings should be validated. Check the calculation is correct and that the AI interpreted the data context correctly.

---

### Q5.5
**What's the best use of AI for campaign performance analysis?**

A) Making final decisions on budget allocation
B) Identifying patterns and generating initial insights for human review
C) Replacing marketing analysts entirely
D) Guaranteeing future performance

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** AI is useful for pattern identification and initial analysis, but humans should review, contextualize, and make decisions.

---

## Section: Report Generation

### Q5.6
**How can AI assist with marketing report narratives?**

A) Writing entire reports without human input
B) Generating draft narratives that explain data findings
C) Replacing all visualizations with text
D) Creating fictional results

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** AI can draft narrative explanations of data findings, which humans then refine with context and recommendations.

---

### Q5.7
**What should always accompany AI-generated insights in reports?**

A) Disclaimers that AI wrote it
B) Source data references and methodology notes
C) Competitor logos
D) Stock photos

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Reports should show where data came from and how analysis was conducted to support credibility and verifiability.

---

### Q5.8
**How should AI be used for executive summaries?**

A) Let AI write the final summary
B) Use AI to draft, then add strategic context and recommendations
C) Skip summaries entirely
D) Use AI-generated summaries without review

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** AI can draft summary structures, but humans should add strategic interpretation, context, and actionable recommendations.

# Module 6: Personalization & Testing (15 questions)

## Section: Segmentation

### Q6.1
**How can AI assist with audience segmentation?**

A) Automatically segment without data
B) Analyze customer data to identify meaningful segment characteristics
C) Replace all customer research
D) Guess customer preferences

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** AI can analyze customer data to identify patterns and suggest segment definitions based on behaviors and attributes.

---

### Q6.2
**What's a persona, and how can AI help create them?**

A) A real customer profile
B) A fictional representation of your ideal customer; AI can draft based on data patterns
C) Your CEO's preferences
D) A competitor analysis

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Personas are fictional composites representing customer segments. AI can help draft persona details based on customer data analysis.

---

### Q6.3
**What should you verify in AI-generated personas?**

A) Only the name
B) That characteristics reflect real customer data and behaviors
C) Spelling of job titles
D) Photo selections

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Personas should be grounded in real data. Verify AI-generated attributes match actual customer research and behaviors.

---

## Section: Content Variants

### Q6.4
**What's the best approach for generating A/B test variants with AI?**

A) Make tiny changes to punctuation
B) Generate meaningfully different approaches while controlling variables
C) Change everything at once
D) Use the same content twice

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Good variants test meaningful differences (different headlines, CTAs, or value propositions) while controlling other variables.

---

### Q6.5
**How many variants should you typically test at once?**

A) As many as possible
B) 2-4 variants to maintain statistical validity and clear insights
C) Only 1
D) 10+

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Testing 2-4 variants allows meaningful comparison while maintaining sample size and clear learnings.

---

### Q6.6
**What makes a good hypothesis for A/B testing?**

A) "Version B will be better"
B) A specific, testable prediction tied to an element change and expected outcome
C) A guess about what might work
D) Whatever the AI suggests

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Good hypotheses are specific: "Changing the CTA from 'Submit' to 'Get My Guide' will increase form completions by 10%."

---

## Section: Testing Framework

### Q6.7
**What is statistical significance in A/B testing?**

A) How important the test is
B) Confidence that results are due to the change, not random chance
C) The size of your sample
D) How long the test runs

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Statistical significance indicates the likelihood that differences in results are due to the tested change rather than random variation.

---

### Q6.8
**How long should you run an A/B test?**

A) Until you see any difference
B) Until you reach statistical significance with adequate sample size
C) Exactly one week
D) One day

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Tests should run until they reach statistical significance with enough samples to account for natural variation.

# Module 7: Automation Workflows (10 questions)

## Section: Workflow Design

### Q7.1
**What's the first step in designing a marketing automation workflow?**

A) Choose your automation tool
B) Define the goal and map the customer journey
C) Start building immediately
D) Write all the email content

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Start with clear goals and understand the customer journey before selecting tools or creating content.

---

### Q7.2
**How can AI assist in workflow design?**

A) Replace all automation tools
B) Help brainstorm triggers, map logic, and draft content for sequences
C) Automatically configure integrations
D) Monitor workflows in real-time

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** AI is useful for brainstorming workflow logic, identifying trigger opportunities, and drafting sequence content.

---

### Q7.3
**What's a "trigger" in marketing automation?**

A) A button users click
B) An event or condition that starts an automated sequence
C) The end of a workflow
D) A type of email

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Triggers are events (form submission, purchase, page visit) that initiate automated actions.

---

## Section: Lead Nurturing

### Q7.4
**What's the purpose of lead scoring?**

A) Grading content quality
B) Prioritizing leads based on their likelihood to convert
C) Counting total leads
D) Measuring email opens

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Lead scoring assigns points based on behaviors and attributes to identify leads most ready for sales engagement.

---

### Q7.5
**How can AI improve lead nurture sequences?**

A) Replace all human decision-making
B) Personalize content, optimize timing, and suggest next best actions
C) Score leads without data
D) Guarantee conversions

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** AI can help personalize nurture content, analyze optimal send times, and suggest appropriate next steps based on engagement.

---

### Q7.6
**What should you monitor in automated email sequences?**

A) Only total sends
B) Engagement metrics at each step, drop-off points, and conversions
C) Just the final conversion
D) Competitor sequences

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Track performance at each step to identify where sequences succeed, where leads drop off, and overall conversion impact.

---

### Q7.7
**When should automation hand off to sales?**

A) After the first email
B) When leads reach scoring thresholds or take high-intent actions
C) Never - automation handles everything
D) Only when they request it

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Sales handoff should occur when leads demonstrate buying intent through scoring thresholds or specific actions.

---

### Q7.8
**What's a best practice for automation content?**

A) Use only promotional messages
B) Vary content types and provide value before asking for action
C) Send identical messages daily
D) Use all caps for urgency

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Effective automation mixes valuable content with promotional messages, building trust before pushing for conversion.

---

### Q7.9
**How should you test marketing automations?**

A) Test in production with real leads
B) Use test contacts through the complete workflow before launching
C) Skip testing to launch faster
D) Only test the first email

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Send test contacts through the entire workflow to verify all triggers, content, timing, and integrations work correctly.

---

### Q7.10
**What's a common mistake in marketing automation?**

A) Using too few emails
B) Over-automating and losing human touch and relevance
C) Sending at the same time daily
D) Having too few workflows

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Over-automation can feel impersonal and miss opportunities for human connection. Balance automation with personalization.
