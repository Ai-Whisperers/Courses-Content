## Answer Key (Instructor Only)

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answers</summary>

### Multiple Choice
1. **B** - Start with seed keywords based on core topics, then expand systematically.
2. **C** - Google emphasizes content demonstrating expertise that satisfies search intent, not keyword-stuffed or search-engine-first content.
3. **C** - Differentiation comes from identifying what competitors miss and providing unique value.
4. **C** - Front-load keywords when possible, use power words, and stay under 60 characters.
5. **C** - Content should be refreshed regularly based on outdated info, new developments, and optimization opportunities.

### True/False
6. **False** - Google does not automatically penalize AI content; it evaluates helpfulness and quality regardless of how content was created.
7. **True** - Understanding search intent is essential for creating content that satisfies user needs.
8. **True** - Keyword clustering organizes related terms into logical groups for content planning.
9. **False** - Meta descriptions should be under 155 characters.
10. **False** - Competitive analysis should identify gaps and differentiation opportunities, not just copy competitors.

### Scenario-Based
11. **B** - Target keywords where you can demonstrate genuine expertise. E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) matters.
12. **B** - Increase keyword usage naturally in strategic positions while maintaining readability. Keyword stuffing hurts quality.
13. **C** - Cover common themes (table stakes) AND add unique angles for differentiation. You can't skip what users expect, but you must add unique value.

### Short Answer
14. The four types of search intent:
    1. Informational
    2. Commercial (or Commercial Investigation)
    3. Transactional
    4. Navigational
