### Time Investment Reality

| Content Type | AI Generation | Human Editing | Total Time | Traditional Time |
|--------------|---------------|---------------|------------|------------------|
| Blog post (1500 words) | 5 min | 45-60 min | ~60 min | 3-4 hours |
| Social post batch (10) | 3 min | 20-30 min | ~30 min | 1-2 hours |
| Email campaign | 5 min | 30-45 min | ~45 min | 2 hours |
| Ad copy (5 variants) | 2 min | 15-20 min | ~20 min | 1 hour |

**Key Insight**: AI doesn't eliminate work; it shifts it from creation to curation and enhancement.

## 2. Blog Post Generation

### The Blog Post Framework

#### Step 1: Create a Content Brief

Before prompting AI, clarify:

```markdown
# Content Brief Template

Topic: [Specific topic]
Target Keyword: [Primary SEO keyword]
Search Intent: [Informational / Transactional / Navigational]
Target Audience: [Who specifically]
Reader's Goal: [What they want to accomplish]
Unique Angle: [What makes this different from existing content]
Word Count: [Target length]
Desired Outcome: [What action should reader take]
```

#### Step 2: Generate Outline First

```
ROLE: Act as a content strategist specializing in [your industry].

CONTEXT:
[Paste your brand context]

BRIEF:
Topic: [Topic]
Target keyword: [Keyword]
Audience: [Audience]
Unique angle: [Your angle]

TASK: Create a detailed blog post outline with:
- Compelling H1 title (include power words)
- 6-8 H2 sections with logical flow
- 2-3 bullet points per section (key points to cover)
- Suggested word count per section
- Questions to answer in each section
- Data/example placeholders

FORMAT: Markdown outline

CONSTRAINTS:
- Address search intent directly
- Include practical, actionable content
- Avoid generic advice - be specific
- Note where original examples should go
```

#### Step 3: Generate Section by Section

Instead of generating the entire post, work section by section:

```
Using the outline we created, write the [Introduction / Section 1 / etc.].

REQUIREMENTS:
- [X] words for this section
- Hook the reader immediately (no "In today's world")
- Include specific examples or data points
- Use conversational but authoritative tone
- End with transition to next section

BRAND VOICE REMINDER:
[Key elements from your brand context]
```

#### Step 4: Enhance with Original Elements

After AI generates content, add:

- **Your unique data**: "In our analysis of 500 campaigns..."
- **Personal experience**: "When we tried this approach..."
- **Expert quotes**: Reach out to industry experts
- **Original examples**: Your case studies or client stories
- **Contrarian takes**: Where you disagree with common advice

### Blog Post Quality Checklist

- [ ] Hook in first 50 words (no generic openings)
- [ ] Clear value proposition by end of introduction
- [ ] Each section has actionable takeaway
- [ ] Original examples or data included
- [ ] Subheadings are scannable and informative
- [ ] Internal links to related content
- [ ] Strong CTA matching reader intent
- [ ] Meta description written
- [ ] Featured image selected/created

## 3. Social Media Content at Scale

### Platform-Specific Considerations

| Platform | Length | Tone | Best Content Types |
|----------|--------|------|-------------------|
| LinkedIn | 150-300 words | Professional, insightful | Stories, tips, industry takes |
| Twitter/X | Under 280 chars | Concise, punchy | Threads, insights, engagement |
| Instagram | 125-150 words | Visual-first, casual | Behind-scenes, tips, stories |
| Facebook | 40-80 words | Friendly, conversational | Questions, shares, community |

### The Batch Content Method

Generate a week's content in one session:

```
ROLE: Act as a social media strategist for B2B companies.

CONTEXT:
[Brand context]

TOPIC THEME: [Weekly theme, e.g., "Customer Success Tips"]

TASK: Create 5 LinkedIn posts for the week, each with a different format:

1. STORY POST - Personal anecdote with business lesson
2. TIP POST - Single actionable tip with context
3. CONTRARIAN POST - Challenge common industry assumption
4. DATA POST - Insight based on statistics or research
5. ENGAGEMENT POST - Question to spark discussion

FORMAT for each post:
- Hook (first line, under 10 words, creates curiosity)
- Body (3-5 short paragraphs, line breaks between)
- Takeaway (clear insight or action)
- CTA (question or invitation)
- Hashtags (3 relevant ones)

CONSTRAINTS:
- Each post 150-250 words
- No emojis in body (optional in CTA only)
- Avoid: "I'm excited to share," "Just wanted to say," "In today's world"
- Include specific numbers or examples where possible
- Write for people scrolling quickly - front-load value
```

### The LinkedIn Hook Formula

Strong hooks that stop the scroll:

```
Pattern: [Unexpected statement] + [Promise of value]

Examples:
- "I lost $50,000 on a marketing campaign. Here's what it taught me:"
- "The best marketers I know do the opposite of this:"
- "After 10 years in marketing, I've learned one thing:"
- "Everyone's talking about [trend]. Here's what they're missing:"
- "I asked 100 CMOs their biggest mistake. One answer dominated:"
```

### Social Media Batch Template

```markdown
# Social Media Batch - Week of [Date]

## Monday: Story Post
Hook: [Compelling first line]
Body: [3-4 paragraphs]
CTA: [Question or invitation]

## Tuesday: Tip Post
Hook: [Problem or opportunity]
Tip: [Specific, actionable advice]
Why it works: [Brief explanation]
CTA: [What to do next]

## Wednesday: Contrarian Post
Hook: [Challenge assumption]
Argument: [Your perspective with evidence]
Nuance: [Acknowledge other side]
CTA: [Ask for opinions]

## Thursday: Data Post
Hook: [Surprising statistic]
Context: [Why this matters]
Insight: [What to do with this information]
CTA: [Apply the insight]

## Friday: Engagement Post
Question: [Thought-provoking question]
Context: [Brief setup]
Your answer: [Share your perspective]
CTA: [Invite responses]
```

## 4. Email Marketing Copy

### Email Types and AI Approaches

| Email Type | AI Strength | Human Focus |
|------------|-------------|-------------|
| Welcome series | Structure, consistency | Brand personality |
| Newsletter | Content summaries, formatting | Curation, voice |
| Promotional | Urgency copy, variations | Offer strategy |
| Nurture sequences | Educational content | Journey mapping |
| Re-engagement | Creative hooks | Segmentation |

### The Email Generation Framework

#### Subject Line Generation

```
Generate 10 subject lines for an email about [topic/offer].

Email context:
- Audience: [Who]
- Goal: [What action]
- Key benefit: [Main value prop]
- Urgency: [If any]

Requirements:
- Under 50 characters each
- Mix of approaches: curiosity, benefit, urgency, personalization
- Avoid spam triggers (FREE, Act Now, etc.)
- Include 2 question-based options
- Include 2 number-based options
```

#### Email Body Framework

```
ROLE: Email copywriter for [industry].

CONTEXT: [Brand context]

EMAIL DETAILS:
- Type: [Welcome / Promotional / Newsletter / etc.]
- Goal: [Primary action]
- Audience segment: [Who specifically]
- Key message: [One sentence summary]

TASK: Write the email body.

STRUCTURE:
1. Opening hook (personal, relevant to reader)
2. Value statement (what's in it for them)
3. Body content (support the value, build interest)
4. Social proof (if applicable)
5. Clear CTA (single, specific action)
6. P.S. line (optional, secondary hook)

CONSTRAINTS:
- Keep under [X] words
- Use "you" more than "we"
- One main CTA only
- Scannable with short paragraphs
- Mobile-friendly (preview text consideration)

AVOID:
- "Hope this email finds you well"
- "I wanted to reach out"
- "Just checking in"
- Multiple competing CTAs
```

### Email Campaign Sequence

For nurture sequences, map the journey first:

```
Create a 5-email nurture sequence for [goal].

AUDIENCE JOURNEY:
Email 1: [Awareness - What problem exists]
Email 2: [Interest - Why it matters]
Email 3: [Consideration - Options available]
Email 4: [Evaluation - Why our solution]
Email 5: [Decision - Clear next step]

For each email, provide:
- Subject line (2 options)
- Preview text
- Email body (~200 words)
- Primary CTA
- Send timing recommendation
```

## 5. Ad Copy and Headlines

### Ad Copy Frameworks

#### PAS Framework (Problem-Agitation-Solution)

```
Generate ad copy using the PAS framework for [product/service].

PRODUCT: [What you're selling]
TARGET: [Who it's for]
KEY PROBLEM: [Main pain point]

Format:
- Problem: State the problem they face
- Agitation: Make them feel the pain
- Solution: Present your offer as the answer

Constraints:
- Primary text: Under 125 characters
- Headline: Under 40 characters
- Include clear CTA
- Create 3 variations
```

#### AIDA Framework (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)

```
Generate ad copy using AIDA for [product/service].

Create 3 variations with:
- Attention: Hook that stops scrolling
- Interest: Relevant benefit
- Desire: Emotional or logical appeal
- Action: Clear next step

Platform: [Facebook / Google / LinkedIn]
Character limits: [Specify per platform]
```

### Headline Generation

```
Generate 20 headlines for [topic/product].

Mix of approaches:
- How-to (5 headlines)
- Numbered lists (5 headlines)
- Question-based (5 headlines)
- Benefit-focused (5 headlines)

Requirements:
- Power words: new, proven, secret, simple, fast
- Specificity: use numbers when possible
- Length: 6-12 words
- Audience: [target reader]
```

## 6. Maintaining Brand Voice

### Creating Your AI Style Guide

Document these elements for consistent AI prompting:

```markdown
# AI Style Guide for [Brand]

## Voice Characteristics

### We ARE:
- [Characteristic 1]: Example of how this sounds
- [Characteristic 2]: Example of how this sounds
- [Characteristic 3]: Example of how this sounds

### We are NOT:
- [Anti-characteristic 1]: What to avoid
- [Anti-characteristic 2]: What to avoid

## Language Preferences

### Words We Use:
| Instead of | We say |
|------------|--------|
| Utilize | Use |
| Leverage | Apply |
| Synergy | Collaboration |
| [Generic] | [Our word] |

### Phrases We Avoid:
- "Industry-leading"
- "Best-in-class"
- "Cutting-edge"
- [Add your own]

### Phrases That Are "Us":
- "[Your phrase 1]"
- "[Your phrase 2]"
- "[Your phrase 3]"

## Formatting Standards

### Sentence Structure:
- Average length: [X] words
- Complexity: [Simple / Moderate / Complex]
- Active voice: [Always / Usually / Sometimes]

### Paragraph Structure:
- Length: [X-X] sentences
- Line breaks: [Frequent / Moderate / Minimal]

### Lists and Headers:
- Bullet style: [Dashes / Dots / Numbers]
- Capitalization: [Sentence / Title case]

## Content Personality

### When we write, we sound like:
"[Example paragraph in your voice]"

### We DO NOT sound like:
"[Example of what to avoid]"

## Quick Voice Check
Before publishing, verify:
- [ ] Sounds like a human, not a corporation
- [ ] Uses our specific terminology
- [ ] Avoids banned words/phrases
- [ ] Matches our energy level
- [ ] Reader can identify it as "us"
```

### Voice Calibration Prompt

Use this to tune AI to your voice:

```
I'm going to train you on my brand voice. Here are 3 examples of our best content:

EXAMPLE 1:
[Paste content]

EXAMPLE 2:
[Paste content]

EXAMPLE 3:
[Paste content]

Based on these examples, describe our brand voice in terms of:
1. Tone (formal to casual scale)
2. Personality traits
3. Common patterns or phrases
4. Sentence structure preferences
5. How we address the reader

Then, write a short paragraph about [topic] in this exact voice.
```

## 7. Avoiding Generic AI Content

### The "Uncanny Valley" Problem

AI content often sounds "almost right but off" because:

- Overuse of certain phrases
- Lack of specific details
- Missing emotional nuance
- Generic examples
- Predictable structure

### The Specificity Injection Method

Transform generic AI content by adding:

| Generic | Specific |
|---------|----------|
| "Many companies struggle with..." | "When we surveyed 200 B2B marketers last quarter..." |
| "This can lead to significant results..." | "This approach increased our client's conversion rate from 2.1% to 4.7%..." |
| "Experts recommend..." | "According to Rand Fishkin's analysis of 10,000 posts..." |
| "A leading company..." | "When HubSpot implemented this in 2023..." |

### The "First Draft" Mindset

Treat AI output as a rough first draft that needs:

1. **Personality injection** - Add humor, opinions, voice
2. **Detail enhancement** - Replace generics with specifics
3. **Structure optimization** - Improve flow for your audience
4. **Value densification** - Remove fluff, add insights
5. **Authenticity check** - Does this sound like you?
