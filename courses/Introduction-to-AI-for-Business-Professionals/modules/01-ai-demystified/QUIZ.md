## Answer Key

### Section 1: Multiple Choice

1. **B** - AI learns from examples rather than following explicit rules
   - *Explanation: This is the fundamental difference between AI and traditional rules-based software.*

2. **C** - Narrow AI (specifically generative)
   - *Explanation: ChatGPT is designed for specific language tasks and generates content.*

3. **B** - AI designed for specific tasks, not general intelligence
   - *Explanation: Narrow AI excels at designated tasks but cannot transfer to other domains.*

4. **C** - High-quality training data
   - *Explanation: AI learns from data; quality data is essential for good results.*

5. **B** - When AI generates confident but false information
   - *Explanation: Hallucination is when AI produces plausible-sounding but incorrect information.*

6. **B** - AI automates tasks within jobs, not entire jobs
   - *Explanation: AI typically handles specific tasks, while humans handle judgment, creativity, and relationships.*

7. **B** - Forecasts outcomes based on patterns in data
   - *Explanation: Predictive AI analyzes historical data to make forecasts.*

8. **B** - AI learns from human data which contains biases
   - *Explanation: Historical biases in training data get encoded into AI systems.*

9. **B** - To set realistic expectations and plan appropriately
   - *Explanation: Understanding limitations helps avoid disappointment and plan for human oversight.*

10. **C** - Identify patterns in large datasets
    - *Explanation: Pattern recognition is a core AI strength.*

### Section 2: True/False

11. **False** - General AI does not exist; all current AI is narrow AI.

12. **False** - Quality matters more than quantity; the right data beats more data.

13. **True** - AI finds correlations; humans must determine causation.

14. **False** - AI tools are increasingly accessible to all industries and company sizes.

15. **True** - AI outputs need human verification due to potential errors and hallucinations.

### Section 3: Scenario-Based

16. **B** - Be skeptical - AI processes patterns, not true understanding
    - *Explanation: AI doesn't truly "understand" - it identifies patterns in data.*

17. **C** - Approve with human review - AI assists, humans verify
    - *Explanation: AI content generation benefits from human oversight for quality and accuracy.*

18. **B** - Recognize this is correlation, not causation; investigate further
    - *Explanation: AI finds correlations; business judgment determines relevance.*

19. **B** - Explain that AI learns from human data which can contain biases
    - *Explanation: Bias enters through training data, not the algorithm itself.*

20. **B** - AI will handle simple queries, humans handle complex ones
    - *Explanation: AI excels at routine tasks; complex situations need human judgment.*
