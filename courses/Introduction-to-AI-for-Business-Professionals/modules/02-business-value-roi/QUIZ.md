## Answer Key

### Section 1: Multiple Choice

1. **B** - Efficiency, revenue, risk reduction, experience
   - *Explanation: These are the four primary ways AI creates business value.*

2. **B** - Implementation plus ongoing operations plus opportunity costs
   - *Explanation: Total cost includes all direct and indirect costs.*

3. **C** - 60%
   - *Explanation: The recommended portfolio is 60% quick wins, 30% optimization, 10% transformation.*

4. **B** - Low-risk projects that deliver fast value with proven tools
   - *Explanation: Quick wins build momentum with minimal risk.*

5. **B** - Data preparation and cleaning
   - *Explanation: Data work is often 60-80% of AI project effort.*

6. **A** - When the solution is core to competitive advantage
   - *Explanation: Build when differentiation justifies the investment.*

7. **B** - Revenue value (through retention)
   - *Explanation: Preventing churn retains revenue that would otherwise be lost.*

8. **B** - 15-20%
   - *Explanation: AI projects often have unexpected costs; contingency protects budgets.*

9. **B** - They build organizational confidence and fund larger initiatives
   - *Explanation: Quick wins create momentum and demonstrate AI value.*

10. **A** - How long until cumulative benefits exceed cumulative costs
    - *Explanation: Payback period shows when investment is recovered.*

### Section 2: True/False

11. **False** - Strategic and qualitative benefits should also be considered, even if not precisely quantified.

12. **True** - Total cost of ownership includes all these ongoing expenses.

13. **False** - Build vs. buy depends on strategic importance and resources.

14. **False** - Other factors like strategic fit, risk, and alternatives also matter.

15. **True** - Quick wins reveal who supports and resists AI adoption.

### Section 3: Scenario-Based

16. **B** - 150%
    - *Calculation: Value = 100 hrs Ã— $50 = $5,000; Cost = $2,000; ROI = ($5,000-$2,000)/$2,000 = 150%*

17. **B** - Buy an existing SaaS tool
    - *Explanation: No internal expertise, tight timeline, good solutions exist = buy.*

18. **B** - Include quantifiable benefits and describe strategic/qualitative benefits separately
    - *Explanation: Present complete picture with appropriate rigor for each benefit type.*

19. **B** - Be skeptical and ask for case studies with verified results
    - *Explanation: Extraordinary claims require evidence; 10x immediate ROI is rare.*

20. **B** - Factor in change management costs and potential adoption challenges
    - *Explanation: Adoption affects whether theoretical ROI becomes reality.*
