## Answer Key

### Section 1: Multiple Choice

1. **C** - The prospect's challenge and how you solve it with key benefits
   - *Executive summaries should be prospect-focused, not company-focused.*

2. **B** - To provide a lower-investment entry point that meets essential needs
   - *Core options help budget-constrained buyers get started.*

3. **C** - The customer's challenge and results are relevant to this prospect's situation
   - *Relevance trumps size or recency.*

4. **C** - Acknowledge them honestly and show how you mitigate or address them
   - *Honesty builds trust; evasion damages it.*

5. **B** - A discovery question designed to surface a competitor's weakness
   - *Landmine questions help the prospect discover issues themselves.*

6. **B** - Verifying accuracy and ensuring personalization reflects discovery findings
   - *Accuracy and personalization are critical; formatting is secondary.*

7. **C** - The prospect's own language from discovery conversations
   - *Using their words shows you listened and understood.*

8. **B** - Understand what "expensive" means to them and explore value or alternatives
   - *Probe before responding; "expensive" can mean many things.*

9. **B** - Because every prospect has unique needs, and personalization shows you listened
   - *Generic proposals signal you didn't understand their situation.*

10. **B** - Answering the question while differentiating and providing proof points
    - *Compliance meets minimum; compelling wins.*

### Section 2: True/False

11. **False** - All AI-generated content requires human review for accuracy and personalization.

12. **False** - Proposals should use more "you" language, focusing on the prospect's needs and benefits.

13. **True** - Multiple options give prospects control and often increase close rates.

14. **False** - Industry-different case studies can be highly relevant if the challenge and results match.

15. **False** - Battle cards should honestly assess competitor strengths and how to address them.

### Section 3: Scenario-Based

16. **B** - Same size and challenge is most relevant
    - *Challenge match often matters more than industry for credibility.*

17. **B** - Change to language the prospect actually used
    - *Using their language shows you listened and avoids putting words in their mouth.*

18. **B** - Explain that without understanding their needs, any proposal would be a guess
    - *Generic proposals waste everyone's time and rarely win.*

19. **B** - Acknowledge their strengths, then focus on your specific advantages for THIS prospect's priorities
    - *Balanced positioning is more credible than attacking competitors.*

20. **B** - Create a custom Option 4 that meets their specific needs
    - *Flexibility to customize shows you're focused on their success.*
