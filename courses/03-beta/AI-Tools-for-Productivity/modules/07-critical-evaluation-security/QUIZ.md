## Answer Key

### Section 1: Multiple Choice

1. **C** - For legal documents, financial reports, or public statements
   - *Explanation: High-stakes content requires the most rigorous verification.*

2. **B** - Specific statistics and citations
   - *Explanation: AI frequently generates plausible but fabricated specific data and sources.*

3. **B** - Be suspicious and verify independently
   - *Explanation: Convenient statistics are often hallucinated; verification is essential.*

4. **B** - AI can inform but humans must decide
   - *Explanation: Strategic decisions require human accountability and contextual judgment.*

5. **B** - Classify the data sensitivity level
   - *Explanation: Classification determines whether and how data can be used with AI.*

6. **B** - Never be input to standard AI tools
   - *Explanation: PII poses significant privacy and compliance risks.*

7. **C** - The responses highlight AI uncertainty and warrant verification
   - *Explanation: Inconsistent factual responses indicate unreliability on that topic.*

8. **B** - Apply conservative principles and get supervisor approval
   - *Explanation: Without policy, err on the side of caution and seek guidance.*

9. **C** - Author review plus peer review
   - *Explanation: Client-facing documents warrant multi-level review.*

10. **C** - To reduce risk of sensitive data exposure
    - *Explanation: Using minimal necessary data limits potential privacy breaches.*

### Section 2: True/False

11. **False** - AI confidence has no correlation with accuracy; confident hallucinations are common.

12. **False** - AI cannot actually keep data private; the data is processed by the AI system.

13. **True** - You bear responsibility for content you use professionally, regardless of source.

14. **False** - Spot-checking is appropriate for lower-stakes content; important documents need thorough verification.

15. **False** - Your professional judgment and experience should override AI when you have relevant context AI lacks.

### Section 3: Scenario-Based

16. **B** - Verify the study exists and check the actual statistic
    - *Explanation: Even reputable-sounding citations may be hallucinated; always verify.*

17. **B** - Ask AI for a template using generic placeholders, then fill in details manually
    - *Explanation: This approach gets AI help while protecting sensitive employee information.*

18. **B** - AI is uncertain; this fact requires independent verification
    - *Explanation: Inconsistent responses on factual matters indicate AI doesn't have reliable information.*

19. **C** - Remove names and emails before inputting, or aggregate/anonymize the data
    - *Explanation: PII should never be input; anonymization allows safe AI analysis.*

20. **B** - Verify all other factual claims since one error indicates potential unreliability
    - *Explanation: Finding one error means the AI may have made others; comprehensive verification is warranted.*
