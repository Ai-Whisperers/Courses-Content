## Answer Key

### Section 1: Multiple Choice

1. **B** - Define the research question clearly
   - *Explanation: Clear questions guide effective research; without them, you gather unfocused information.*

2. **B** - Verify with current industry reports or official sources
   - *Explanation: AI market statistics are often outdated or hallucinated; always verify with authoritative sources.*

3. **B** - Provide key themes from each interview and ask for pattern analysis
   - *Explanation: Structured input helps AI identify cross-interview patterns effectively.*

4. **B** - Publicly available information you provide to AI
   - *Explanation: AI analysis is only as good as the input; verified public information you provide is most reliable.*

5. **C** - Break into sections and ask AI to synthesize across sections
   - *Explanation: Sectional processing with synthesis allows comprehensive analysis of long documents.*

6. **B** - They make qualitative data specific enough to guide decisions
   - *Explanation: Personas translate research into actionable, memorable representations.*

7. **B** - Key signals, information categories, and red flags
   - *Explanation: Comprehensive frameworks capture meaningful competitive movements.*

8. **C** - Whether it's corroborated by multiple sources and verifiable
   - *Explanation: Corroboration and verifiability, not AI confidence, indicate reliability.*

9. **B** - Specify audience, content requirements, and format structure
   - *Explanation: Specific requirements produce more useful, targeted knowledge articles.*

10. **B** - Indication of what needs verification due to AI knowledge cutoffs
    - *Explanation: Trend analysis must acknowledge AI limitations around recent developments.*

### Section 2: True/False

11. **False** - Most AI tools don't have real-time data access; they have knowledge cutoffs.

12. **True** - AI excels at finding patterns across qualitative data that humans might miss.

13. **False** - Future direction signals are important for anticipating competitive moves.

14. **True** - Specific personas are more actionable than generic descriptions.

15. **False** - Documenting research creates institutional knowledge and enables future reference.

### Section 3: Scenario-Based

16. **B** - Verify with current market research reports before using
    - *Explanation: Market statistics from AI should always be verified with authoritative current sources.*

17. **B** - Report both themes exist with equal representation
    - *Explanation: Honest synthesis includes contradictions; forcing artificial consensus misrepresents findings.*

18. **B** - Verify the feature exists through the competitor's official materials
    - *Explanation: AI can hallucinate competitor features; verify before strategic decisions.*

19. **B** - Create three different summaries focusing on what each audience cares about
    - *Explanation: Different audiences need different framings of the same information.*

20. **B** - Use AI for fundamentals and verify recent developments through current sources
    - *Explanation: AI provides foundation understanding; current sources fill in recent developments.*
