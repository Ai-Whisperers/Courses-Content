## Answer Key

### Section 1: Multiple Choice

1. **B** - Define the meeting objective and desired outcomes
   - *Explanation: The preparation workflow starts with defining purpose before creating agendas or gathering materials.*

2. **B** - Owner, task description, and deadline
   - *Explanation: Accountability requires knowing who, what, and when.*

3. **C** - Within 24 hours of the meeting
   - *Explanation: Prompt follow-up maintains momentum and ensures clarity while discussion is fresh.*

4. **C** - Executive updates are brief and outcome-focused; team updates are more detailed
   - *Explanation: Executives need quick summaries; teams need operational detail.*

5. **C** - The audience, duration, and key message
   - *Explanation: These fundamentals shape everything else in the presentation.*

6. **C** - Structure into standard sections (decisions, action items, next steps)
   - *Explanation: Standard structure makes notes scannable and actionable while preserving key information.*

7. **B** - Adapted for style but consistent in facts
   - *Explanation: Facts must be consistent; tone and detail level should match the channel.*

8. **C** - Conversational talking points and transitions
   - *Explanation: Speaker notes guide natural delivery, not reading verbatim.*

9. **B** - Anticipated concerns, your responses, and questions to ask
   - *Explanation: Briefings prepare you for the conversation, not just provide background.*

10. **C** - Increase urgency gradually while remaining professional
    - *Explanation: Professional escalation maintains relationships while ensuring action.*

### Section 2: True/False

11. **False** - Standard AI tools cannot attend meetings; they can only process notes you provide (specialized tools like Otter.ai can transcribe).

12. **True** - Owner and deadline are essential for accountability and tracking.

13. **False** - Executive summaries should be concise with key points only; full details belong in the complete documentation.

14. **False** - Different stakeholders need different levels of detail and different focus areas.

15. **True** - Speaker notes should prompt natural speaking, not be a script to read.

### Section 3: Scenario-Based

16. **B** - It's missing who is responsible and specific deadline
    - *Explanation: "Follow up on the project" lacks specificity about owner, timing, and what "follow up" means.*

17. **B** - The level of detail and focus area
    - *Explanation: Facts remain consistent, but executives need impact summary, team needs actions, client needs reassurance.*

18. **B** - Decision criteria, options overview, and time for discussion
    - *Explanation: Decision meetings need clear criteria and options to enable productive discussion.*

19. **B** - Remove the joke and ensure professional tone for client communication
    - *Explanation: Client communications should be professional; humor is risky in written form.*

20. **B** - "John to confirm exact budget figure"
    - *Explanation: Approximate figures should become action items to confirm, not stated as facts.*
