## Answer Key

### Section 1: Multiple Choice

1. **B** - AI predicts likely next words based on patterns, not truth
   - *Explanation: LLMs generate text by predicting probable next tokens based on training patterns, without access to truth verification.*

2. **B** - Claude because of its large context window
   - *Explanation: Claude offers context windows of 100K-200K tokens, making it well-suited for very long documents.*

3. **C** - The date after which events are not in the AI's training data
   - *Explanation: AI models are trained on data up to a certain date; they don't know about events after that cutoff.*

4. **B** - Drafting and restructuring text content
   - *Explanation: AI excels at text generation and transformation tasks, while factual accuracy and decision-making require human verification.*

5. **B** - Review and verify the content before using it
   - *Explanation: AI outputs are starting points that require human review for accuracy, appropriateness, and context.*

6. **B** - Transparency - your voice and review should be in the final content
   - *Explanation: Professional ethics requires that you take ownership of content and ensure it represents your professional judgment.*

7. **B** - The amount of text an AI can process at once
   - *Explanation: Context window refers to the token limit for input and output in a single interaction.*

8. **C** - When processing data containing personal information
   - *Explanation: Personal and confidential information requires extra caution due to privacy concerns and data policies.*

9. **C** - Multiple responses indicate AI uncertainty; verify important facts
   - *Explanation: Inconsistent responses highlight the probabilistic nature of AI; critical information should be independently verified.*

10. **B** - Different tools have different strengths for different tasks
    - *Explanation: Each AI tool has unique capabilities, context windows, and integrations suited to different use cases.*

### Section 2: True/False

11. **False** - Most AI tools don't have real-time internet access unless specifically enabled through features like web browsing plugins.

12. **False** - AI confidence and writing quality do not correlate with factual accuracy; hallucinations often sound very confident.

13. **False** - You remain accountable for any content you use professionally, regardless of how it was created.

14. **True** - Iterative refinement is an effective approach, though starting with specific prompts often produces better initial results.

15. **True** - Company policies should always guide what data can be input into external AI tools.

### Section 3: Scenario-Based

16. **B** - Explain that AI may not have current information and offer to use web search instead
    - *Explanation: Acknowledging AI limitations while providing alternatives demonstrates both understanding and helpfulness.*

17. **B** - Explain you used AI assistance to draft it, then reviewed and edited
    - *Explanation: Transparency about AI use while noting your review and editing is the ethical approach.*

18. **B** - Use generic industry terms instead of specific client information
    - *Explanation: Maintaining confidentiality while still using AI productively by generalizing sensitive details.*

19. **C** - Verify the source and accuracy of this statistic independently
    - *Explanation: AI-generated statistics should always be verified as they may be hallucinated or inaccurate.*

20. **B** - Company data policies and security requirements
    - *Explanation: Security and compliance must be the first consideration before evaluating features or cost.*
