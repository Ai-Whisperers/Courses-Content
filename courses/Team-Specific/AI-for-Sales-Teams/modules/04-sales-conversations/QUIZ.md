## Answer Key

### Section 1: Multiple Choice

1. **B** - To understand the prospect's context and plan the conversation strategically
   - *Preparation enables relevant, focused conversations.*

2. **C** - To quantify the impact of problems and build urgency
   - *Implication questions make pain points concrete and urgent.*

3. **B** - Timing/stalling objection
   - *"Need to think about it" is often a delay tactic or hidden objection.*

4. **B** - Acknowledge the concern genuinely
   - *Acknowledgment builds trust before addressing the concern.*

5. **C** - Within 2 hours while conversation is fresh
   - *Quick follow-up shows professionalism and keeps momentum.*

6. **B** - Set an agenda and confirm time available
   - *Agenda-setting creates structure and respects their time.*

7. **D** - Need-payoff questions
   - *Need-payoff questions have prospects articulate the value of solving.*

8. **B** - Summarizing discussion points and confirming agreed next steps
   - *Follow-ups reinforce value and maintain momentum.*

9. **B** - Acknowledge, probe to understand, respond honestly, and follow up if needed
   - *It's okay not to have all answers; handle professionally.*

10. **B** - To provide quick reference during the call without looking distracted
    - *One-pagers enable in-call reference without losing engagement.*

### Section 2: True/False

11. **False** - The prospect should talk more; 60-70% prospect talking is ideal in discovery.

12. **True** - Preparation for unexpected objections builds confidence and professionalism.

13. **False** - CRM notes should include next steps, action items, and opportunity updates.

14. **True** - Different roles have different priorities and should get tailored questions.

15. **False** - Acknowledge means showing you understand their concern, not that you agree with it.

### Section 3: Scenario-Based

16. **B** - "That's great to hear. What do you like most about it? And if you could improve anything, what would it be?"
    - *Probe to find gaps even in satisfied customers.*

17. **B** - Summary of their key interests, demo highlights relevant to them, and clear next step
    - *Personalized summary with clear CTA maintains momentum.*

18. **B** - Ask what budget they're working with and what "expensive" means to them
    - *Probe before responding to understand the real concern.*

19. **B** - Review previous call notes, identify open items, and plan how to advance the opportunity
    - *Build on previous conversation; don't start over.*

20. **B** - Probe for specificity: "What specific information would be most helpful? And after you review it, what would be our next step?"
    - *Vague next steps rarely close; get specificity.*
