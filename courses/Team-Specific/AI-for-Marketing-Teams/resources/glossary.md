# AI for Marketing - Glossary

## A

**A/B Testing**: Comparing two versions of marketing content to determine which performs better with a specific metric.

**AIDA**: A copywriting framework: Attention, Interest, Desire, Action.

**AI Hallucination**: When AI generates false or fabricated information that appears plausible but is incorrect.

**Automation Workflow**: A series of automated actions triggered by specific conditions or events.

---

## B

**Batch Content**: Creating multiple pieces of content in a single session for efficiency.

**Brand Voice**: The consistent personality and tone expressed in all brand communications.

**Buyer Journey**: The process customers go through from awareness to purchase decision.

---

## C

**Click-Through Rate (CTR)**: Percentage of people who click on a link after seeing it.

**Content Brief**: A document outlining requirements and guidelines for content creation.

**Content Cluster**: A group of related content pieces organized around a central topic.

**Conversion Rate**: Percentage of visitors who complete a desired action.

---

## D

**Dynamic Content**: Content that automatically changes based on recipient data or behavior.

**Disclosure**: Informing audiences when AI has been used to create content.

---

## E

**E-E-A-T**: Google's quality criteria: Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness.

**Engagement Rate**: Measure of how actively involved with content an audience is.

---

## F

**First-Party Data**: Data collected directly from your own customers and audience.

**Funnel**: The stages a prospect moves through from awareness to conversion.

---

## G

**Generative AI**: AI systems that can create new content (text, images, video) based on training data.

---

## H

**Hook**: The opening line of content designed to capture attention and encourage continued reading.

**Helpful Content**: Google's guideline emphasizing content that genuinely serves user needs.

---

## I

**Intent (Search)**: The purpose behind a user's search query (informational, commercial, transactional, navigational).

---

## K

**Keyword Clustering**: Grouping related keywords into topics for content planning.

**Knowledge Cutoff**: The date after which an AI model has no training data.

---

## L

**Landing Page**: A standalone page designed for a specific conversion goal.

**Lead Scoring**: Assigning numerical values to leads based on their likelihood to convert.

**Long-tail Keywords**: Longer, more specific search phrases with lower volume but higher intent.

---

## M

**Meta Description**: The HTML tag that provides a brief summary of page content for search results.

**MQL (Marketing Qualified Lead)**: A lead deemed more likely to become a customer based on engagement.

---

## N

**Nurture Sequence**: A series of communications designed to build relationships with leads over time.

---

## O

**Open Rate**: Percentage of email recipients who open an email.

---

## P

**PAS**: A copywriting framework: Problem, Agitation, Solution.

**Personalization**: Tailoring content to individual users based on their data and behavior.

**Prompt**: The text input given to an AI system to generate a response.

**Prompt Engineering**: The practice of crafting effective prompts to get desired AI outputs.

---

## R

**REVIEW Framework**: Read, Evaluate accuracy, Verify brand voice, Improve clarity, Enhance with expertise, Watch for AI tells.

**ROI (Return on Investment)**: The ratio of gain from an investment relative to its cost.

---

## S

**Segmentation**: Dividing an audience into distinct groups based on shared characteristics.

**SERP**: Search Engine Results Page - what appears when you search.

**SQL (Sales Qualified Lead)**: A lead vetted by sales as ready for direct engagement.

---

## T

**Title Tag**: The HTML element that specifies the title of a web page (shown in search results).

**Trigger**: An event or condition that initiates an automated workflow.

---

## U

**User-Generated Content (UGC)**: Content created by customers rather than the brand.

---

## V

**Voice Calibration**: Training AI on your specific brand voice using examples.

---

## W

**Workflow Automation**: Using technology to perform repetitive tasks automatically.
