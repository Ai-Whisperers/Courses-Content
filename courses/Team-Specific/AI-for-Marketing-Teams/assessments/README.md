# AI for Marketing Teams - Assessment Guide

## Overview

This document provides comprehensive guidance on assessments, grading, and academic policies for the AI for Marketing Teams course.

---

## Assessment Components

| Component | Weight | Quantity | Due Date |
|-----------|--------|----------|----------|
| Module Exercises | 35% | 7 exercises | End of each module |
| Module Quizzes | 20% | 7 quizzes | Before next module |
| Portfolio Pieces | 15% | 10+ pieces | Throughout course |
| Final Project | 30% | 1 project | Week 7 |
| **Total** | **100%** | | |

---

## Module Exercises (35%)

### Purpose
Apply concepts through hands-on marketing content creation and analysis.

### Format
- Located in each module's `EXERCISE.md`
- Duration: 1.5-3 hours per exercise
- Real marketing deliverables

### Grading Criteria
- Quality of Output: 30%
- AI Usage Effectiveness: 25%
- Brand Consistency: 20%
- Process Documentation: 15%
- Completeness: 10%

### Exercise Overview

| Module | Exercise Focus | Key Deliverables |
|--------|---------------|------------------|
| 1 | AI Toolkit Setup | Brand context, first content |
| 2 | Content Batch Creation | Blog post, 5 social posts, 3 emails |
| 3 | SEO Optimization | Keyword research, content brief |
| 4 | Visual Content | 4 graphics, blog header, presentation visuals |
| 5 | Analytics | Data analysis, reports, insights |
| 6 | Personalization | Segments, personalized content, A/B tests |
| 7 | Automation | Workflow design, lead scoring, ROI |

---

## Module Quizzes (20%)

### Purpose
Validate understanding of key concepts and best practices.

### Format
- 10-15 questions per quiz
- Time limit: 15-25 minutes
- Question types: Multiple choice, True/False, Scenario-based

### Policies
- Open notes allowed
- AI tools NOT allowed
- Unlimited attempts with 24-hour delay
- Must pass (70%) before proceeding

### Quiz Topics by Module

| Module | Quiz Focus |
|--------|------------|
| 1 | AI capabilities, ethics, tool selection |
| 2 | Content creation, brand voice, workflows |
| 3 | SEO principles, keyword research, optimization |
| 4 | Visual design, AI image tools, legal considerations |
| 5 | Analytics, reporting, insight extraction |
| 6 | Segmentation, personalization, A/B testing |
| 7 | Automation, lead scoring, ROI measurement |

---

## Portfolio Pieces (15%)

### Purpose
Demonstrate practical AI marketing skills through real deliverables.

### Requirements
Minimum 10 pieces including:
- 3+ blog posts or long-form content
- 5+ social media posts
- 2+ email campaigns
- Visual content samples

### Grading Criteria
- Quality and polish: 40%
- Variety of content types: 20%
- Brand voice consistency: 20%
- AI usage documentation: 20%

---

## Final Project (30%)

### Purpose
Synthesize all learning into a comprehensive AI marketing strategy.

### Deliverables
1. Strategy Document (15-25 pages)
2. Prompt Library (20+ prompts)
3. Content Portfolio (10+ pieces)
4. Video Presentation (10 minutes)

### Grading Breakdown
- Strategy Document Quality: 30%
- Implementation Realism: 20%
- Content Portfolio: 20%
- Prompt Library: 15%
- Presentation: 15%

---

## Grading Scale

| Grade | Percentage | Description |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| A | 90-100% | Exceeds all expectations |
| B | 80-89% | Meets all expectations |
| C | 70-79% | Meets minimum requirements |
| F | Below 70% | Does not meet requirements |

### Passing Requirements
- Overall score: 70%+
- All exercises submitted
- Final project completed
- All quizzes passed (70% minimum)

## Submission Guidelines

### What to Submit
1. Completed deliverables per exercise/project
2. AI interaction logs showing prompt iterations
3. Reflection on process (where required)

### Format Requirements
- Documents: PDF or Markdown
- Images: PNG or JPG
- Videos: MP4 or link to hosted video
- File naming: `ModuleX_ExerciseName_YourName`

### Where to Submit
- Course portal submission system
- One submission per assignment

## AI Usage Policy

### Required
- Use AI tools for all exercises
- Document AI interactions
- Show iteration and refinement

### Allowed
- Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini for content
- Canva, DALL-E for visuals
- Any tool covered in course

### Not Allowed
- AI for quiz answers
- Submitting raw AI output without editing
- Not disclosing AI usage

---

## Academic Integrity

### Expectations
- All work demonstrates your editorial judgment
- AI is a tool, not a replacement for thinking
- Document your process honestly

### Violations
- Copying others' work
- Using AI for quizzes
- Submitting without review/editing
- Falsifying AI interaction logs

### Consequences
- First offense: Warning + redo assignment
- Second offense: Zero on assignment
- Third offense: Course failure

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I use tools not covered in the course?**
A: Yes, but document your usage and ensure quality standards.

**Q: What if AI gives me bad output?**
A: That's expected! Document the iteration process.

**Q: How detailed should AI logs be?**
A: Include prompts, key iterations, and final output. Not every exchange.

**Q: Can I work with others?**
A: Discussion is fine; final work must be your own.

**Q: What if I miss a quiz deadline?**
A: Quizzes have unlimited attempts; complete before next module.
