# Example: AI-Assisted Blog Post

## Project Details

- **Topic**: How to Use AI for Content Marketing Without Losing Your Brand Voice
- **Target Keyword**: AI content marketing
- **Word Count Target**: 1,500 words
- **Audience**: Marketing managers at B2B companies

---

```

CONTEXT:

Target: Marketing managers at tech startups (50-200 employees)

TOPIC: How to use AI for content marketing without losing brand voice
TARGET KEYWORD: AI content marketing
AUDIENCE: Marketing managers worried about AI making their content generic

TASK: Create a detailed blog post outline with:

- 6-8 H2 sections with logical flow
- 3 bullet points per section
- Questions to answer in each section
- Where to add examples (mark with [EXAMPLE])

```

## Step 3: AI-Generated Introduction (Raw)

```
In today's fast-paced digital landscape, content marketers face an unprecedented
challenge. The demand for content has never been higher, yet resources remain
limited. Enter artificial intelligence – a game-changing technology that promises
to revolutionize how we create content. But here's the million-dollar question:
can you leverage AI content marketing without your brand voice getting lost in
the algorithmic shuffle?

The answer is yes, but it requires a strategic approach. In this comprehensive
guide, we'll explore how to harness the power of AI while maintaining the
authentic voice that makes your brand uniquely yours.
```

## Step 4: Edited Introduction (Final)

```
Last quarter, we doubled our content output. Zero new hires.

But here's what we didn't do: publish a single piece of generic,
obviously-AI-written content.

The "AI content marketing" conversation usually goes one of two ways.
Either it's breathless enthusiasm about 10x productivity, or it's hand-wringing
about robots replacing creativity. Both miss the point.

The real question isn't whether to use AI. It's whether you can use it
without your content sounding like everyone else's.

After 18 months of testing AI across 200+ client projects, we've developed
a system that works. Not "kinda works" – actually works. Content that performs,
maintains brand voice, and doesn't make readers cringe.

Here's exactly how we do it.
```

---

## Step 5: What Changed (Annotations)

| Original Element | Change Made | Why |
|------------------|-------------|-----|
| "In today's fast-paced digital landscape" | Deleted | Classic AI tell, adds nothing |
| Generic opening | Specific result + intrigue | Hooks reader with concrete outcome |
| "game-changing technology" | Removed | Cliché, overused |
| "comprehensive guide" | Removed | Generic promise |
| "harness the power" | Removed | AI cliché |
| No data | Added "18 months, 200+ projects" | Establishes credibility with specifics |
| Formal tone | Conversational, direct | Matches brand voice |
| Long sentences | Short, punchy sentences | Better readability |

---

## Step 6: Key Learnings

### What Worked:
- Section-by-section generation gave better control
- Including brand voice descriptors improved output
- Asking for specific examples marked where to add them

### What Needed Heavy Editing:
- Introduction (always needs human touch)
- Transitions between sections
- Generic phrases throughout

### Time Investment:
- AI generation: 15 minutes
- Editing and enhancement: 45 minutes
- Total: 60 minutes (vs. 3-4 hours from scratch)

### The 60/40 Rule in Action:
- AI provided ~60% of structure and first-draft content
- Human added ~40% in editing, examples, and unique insights
- That 40% is what makes it ours
