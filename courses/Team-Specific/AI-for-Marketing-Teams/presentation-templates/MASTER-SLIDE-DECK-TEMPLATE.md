## Module 1: AI Fundamentals

### Slide 1.1 - Title
**AI Fundamentals for Marketers**
- Module 1 of 8
- Duration: 2-3 hours
- Objective: Understand AI capabilities and build quality control habits

### Slide 1.2 - The Marketing AI Landscape
**What We're Working With in 2024**
- Large Language Models (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini)
- Image generators (DALL-E, Midjourney)
- Specialized marketing tools
- Integration capabilities

### Slide 1.3 - How AI Actually Works
**The 30-Second Explanation**
- Trained on massive text data
- Predicts "most likely" next words
- No true understanding
- Pattern matching at scale

[Visual: Simple neural network diagram]

### Slide 1.4 - What AI Does Well
**AI Strengths**
- First drafts and outlines
- Variations and alternatives
- Summarization and extraction
- Research synthesis
- Format conversion

### Slide 1.5 - Where AI Falls Short
**AI Limitations**
- Factual accuracy (hallucinations)
- True creativity and originality
- Brand voice without training
- Strategic thinking
- Emotional intelligence

### Slide 1.6 - The Human-AI Partnership
**Best Results Come From Collaboration**
- Human: Strategy, creativity, judgment
- AI: Execution, speed, scale
- Together: Quality at velocity

[Visual: Venn diagram of human + AI overlap]

### Slide 1.7 - AI Tools for Marketers
**Your Toolkit**

| Tool | Best For | Cost |
|------|----------|------|
| ChatGPT | General content, versatility | Free/$20/mo |
| Claude | Long-form, nuanced writing | Free/$20/mo |
| Jasper | Marketing-specific | $49/mo+ |
| Copy.ai | Quick copy variants | Free/$49/mo |

### Slide 1.8 - ChatGPT Overview
**Key Features**
- GPT-4 for complex tasks
- Web browsing capability
- Image generation (DALL-E)
- Custom GPTs
- Large context window

### Slide 1.9 - Claude Overview
**Key Features**
- Excellent for long documents
- Strong at nuanced writing
- Project/artifact features
- Thoughtful, detailed responses
- Good at following instructions

### Slide 1.10 - Choosing Your Tool
**Decision Framework**
- Quick tasks â†’ ChatGPT
- Long-form content â†’ Claude
- Marketing-specific â†’ Jasper
- Visual content â†’ DALL-E/Midjourney
- All-in-one â†’ Test both, pick one

### Slide 1.11 - The REVIEW Framework
**Quality Control for AI Content**
- **R**ead the full output
- **E**valuate accuracy
- **V**erify brand voice
- **I**mprove clarity
- **E**nhance with expertise
- **W**atch for AI tells

### Slide 1.12 - R: Read the Full Output
**Don't skim - actually read**
- AI buries errors in the middle
- Check every section
- Note what needs changing
- Spot structural issues

### Slide 1.13 - E: Evaluate Accuracy
**Verify facts and claims**
- Statistics need sources
- Never trust AI-generated URLs
- Check dates and names
- Mark "[VERIFY]" for fact-checking

### Slide 1.14 - V: Verify Brand Voice
**Does it sound like you?**
- Check against brand guidelines
- Read aloud - does it flow?
- Would your team write this?
- Consistency across pieces

### Slide 1.15 - I: Improve Clarity
**Make it better**
- Cut unnecessary words
- Simplify complex sentences
- Add transitions
- Break up long paragraphs

### Slide 1.16 - E: Enhance with Expertise
**Add your unique value**
- Insert your examples
- Add industry insights
- Include customer stories
- Share your perspective

### Slide 1.17 - W: Watch for AI Tells
**Remove robotic indicators**
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "It's important to note that..."
- Perfect parallel structure
- Over-use of "crucial," "vital"
- Generic examples

### Slide 1.18 - Exercise Setup
**Your First AI Marketing Task**
- TIME: 20 minutes
- TASK: Generate and review content
- TOOLS: ChatGPT or Claude
- DELIVERABLE: Edited final version

### Slide 1.19 - Demo
**Live Demonstration**
- Write the prompt
- Generate content
- Apply REVIEW framework
- Show editing process

### Slide 1.20 - Module 1 Recap
**Key Takeaways**
1. AI is a powerful assistant, not replacement
2. Choose tools based on task type
3. REVIEW framework ensures quality
4. Human judgment remains essential
5. Practice improves results

## Module 2: Content Creation (Sample Slides)

### Slide 2.1 - Title
**Content Creation with AI**
- Module 2 of 8
- Duration: 3-4 hours
- Objective: Create blog, social, and email content efficiently

### Slide 2.2 - The Content Challenge
**Marketing Teams Face:**
- Growing content demands
- Shrinking resources
- Quality expectations
- Speed requirements
- Multi-channel complexity

### Slide 2.3 - The AI Content Workflow
**Process Overview**
1. Define objective and audience
2. Provide context to AI
3. Generate initial draft
4. Apply REVIEW framework
5. Edit and enhance
6. Publish and measure

[Visual: Workflow diagram]

### Slide 2.4 - Blog Content Workflow
**From Idea to Published Post**
- Outline generation (10 min)
- Section drafting (30 min)
- Human editing (30 min)
- SEO optimization (15 min)
- Final review (15 min)

Total: ~2 hours vs. 4+ traditional

### Slide 2.5 - Effective Blog Prompts
**Prompt Structure**
```
ROLE: [Expert type]
CONTEXT: [Brand, audience, goal]
TASK: [Specific request]
FORMAT: [Structure requirements]
CONSTRAINTS: [Limitations, voice]
```

### Slide 2.6 - Social Media at Scale
**Batch Creation Process**
- Plan content themes weekly
- Generate batches by platform
- Edit for consistency
- Schedule strategically
- Track performance

### Slide 2.7 - Platform Requirements
**Tailor Your Approach**

| Platform | Length | Tone | Best Content |
|----------|--------|------|--------------|
| LinkedIn | 150-300 words | Professional | Insights, tips |
| Twitter/X | <280 chars | Concise | Hooks, threads |
| Instagram | 150 words | Casual | Stories, visuals |
| Facebook | 100-200 words | Friendly | Community |

### Slide 2.8 - Email Marketing with AI
**What AI Can Generate**
- Subject line variations
- Body copy drafts
- Personalization variants
- Nurture sequences
- Re-engagement campaigns

**Content Creation Workshop**
- TIME: 45 minutes
- TASK: Create content for 3 channels
- DELIVERABLES:
  - 1 blog outline
