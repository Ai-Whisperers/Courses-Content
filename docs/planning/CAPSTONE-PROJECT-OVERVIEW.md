# Capstone Project Overview
## FPUNA Summer 2026 - Weeks 9-10 (Final Project)

**Duration**: 20 hours (2 weeks)  
**Weight**: 40% of final grade  
**Format**: Individual project with team presentations  
**Prerequisites**: All Core Modules and Track Modules completed

---

## Overview

The Capstone Project is your opportunity to demonstrate mastery of AI-augmented development by building a complete, production-ready application. You'll apply everything learned in the 10-week program to create a real-world project that showcases your skills to potential employers.

---

## Project Goals

### Technical Goals
1. **Build** a complete application using your specialization track skills
2. **Demonstrate** effective use of OpenCode throughout development
3. **Implement** professional development practices (testing, CI/CD, documentation)
4. **Deploy** to production with proper monitoring
5. **Present** your work professionally

### Learning Goals
1. **Apply** AI-augmented development workflows
2. **Solve** real-world problems independently
3. **Make** technical decisions and justify them
4. **Document** your development process
5. **Collaborate** through code review and feedback

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I work with a partner?**  
A: Projects are individual, but you can help each other debug and review code.

**Q: What if I can't finish all planned features?**  
A: Submit what you have. It's better to have fewer features working perfectly than many half-done features.

**Q: Can I use external libraries?**  
A: Yes, but document why you chose them and ensure they're properly licensed.

**Q: What if my deployment fails?**  
A: Contact instructors immediately. We have backup deployment options.

**Q: Can I switch project ideas mid-week?**  
A: Switching after Day 2 is discouraged. Choose carefully during planning.

**Q: How much should I use OpenCode?**  
A: Use it extensively, but understand the code it generates. Document your usage.

**Q: What if I find a bug during presentation?**  
A: Acknowledge it and explain how you'd fix it. Debugging is part of development.

## Success Tips

1. **Start Early**: Don't wait until Week 9 to think about your project
2. **Scope Appropriately**: Better to do fewer features well than many poorly
3. **Commit Often**: Small, frequent commits are better than large ones
4. **Test Continuously**: Don't wait until the end to test
5. **Document as You Go**: Don't leave documentation for the last day
6. **Use OpenCode Effectively**: Learn to write good prompts
7. **Ask for Help**: Don't struggle alone when stuck
8. **Deploy Early**: Deploy Week 9 Day 1 and iterate
9. **Practice Your Demo**: A smooth demo makes a great impression
10. **Have Fun**: This is your chance to build something you're proud of!
